Do's and Don'ts: How to Identify Fake News

✅ DO:

  • Check the source: Is it from an official company website or verified news outlet?
  • Look for confirmations: Are other reputable sources reporting the same story?
  • Verify URLs: Do the links match the claimed source? Watch for URL mismatches.
  • Check official announcements: Search for official press releases or statements from the company
  • Assess feasibility: Does the claim align with the company's business model and capabilities?
  • Check publication dates: Real news has specific, verifiable dates and timestamps
  • Look for credentials: Is the author/source named and credible?

❌ DON'T:

  • Don't trust single sources: Always cross-reference with multiple credible outlets
  • Don't ignore URL mismatches: If the URL doesn't match the claimed source, it's a red flag
  • Don't assume vague claims are true: Real news provides specific details, dates, and locations
  • Don't skip fact-checking: Take 30 seconds to verify before sharing
  • Don't ignore business logic: Consider if the claim makes sense for the company
  • Don't share without verification: Misinformation spreads fastest when people don't verify first
  • Don't trust sensational headlines: Fake news often uses exaggeration and emotional language
